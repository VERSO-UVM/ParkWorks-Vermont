Tools, Templates, and Resources

Access helpful templates, guides, case studies, and tools to support your park development journey in Vermont.

Vermont State Resources

  • Vermont Department of Forests, Parks and Recreation: State agency overseeing Vermont's parks and recreation programs
  • Vermont Agency of Commerce and Community Development: Resources for community development and planning
  • Vermont Recreation and Parks Association (VRPA): Professional association for Vermont recreation professionals; includes grant opportunities and peer network
  • Vermont League of Cities and Towns: Resources and advocacy for municipal governments
  • Regional Planning Commissions: Technical assistance and planning support across Vermont's regions
  • Vermont Urban and Community Forestry: Resources for tree planting and urban forestry
  • VT Department of Environmental Conservation - Planning: Environmental guidelines and permitting requirements

National Organizations

  • National Recreation and Park Association (NRPA): Professional development, research, and advocacy with extensive park planning resources
  • American Society of Landscape Architects (ASLA): Design resources and professional directory
  • Project for Public Spaces (PPS): Placemaking resources and best practices for community spaces
  • Trust for Public Land: Conservation finance and park planning tools, plus grant writing guides
  • Rails-to-Trails Conservancy: Trail development resources and federal funding guidance
  • KaBOOM!: Playground design and community build resources

Planning and Design Tools

Downloadable Templates:

  • Community Survey Template: Sample surveys for gathering community input (Word/Google Forms)
  • Park Needs Assessment Form: Standardized form for evaluating community recreation needs
  • Master Plan Outline: Template for comprehensive park planning documents with planning committee checklist
  • Design Checklist: ADA accessibility and safety verification checklist
  • Budget Templates: Sample budgets for various park project sizes ($30K-$500K)
  • RFP Template: Request for proposal for hiring landscape architects, engineers, and contractors
  • Maintenance Checklists: Daily, weekly, and seasonal maintenance schedules
  • Staffing Plan Template: Roles, hours, and annual cost projections
  • Asset Inventory Spreadsheet: Track equipment, replacement costs, and maintenance schedules

All templates available in editable formats (Word/Excel/Google Docs) - Contact us to request specific templates.

Technical Guides & Standards

  • Playground Safety Standards: Complete CPSC and ASTM safety guidelines with fall protection requirements
  • Trail Design Manual: Best practices for trail construction, materials, and maintenance in Vermont climates
  • Sports Field Maintenance: Guides for athletic field care and renovation
  • Native Plant Guides: Vermont native species selection and ecological value information
  • Stormwater Management: Green infrastructure design resources including rain gardens and permeable surfaces
  • ADA Compliance Guide: Accessibility standards for all park features with technical specifications

Case Studies: Vermont Success Stories

Learn from communities across Vermont that have successfully developed parks and recreation facilities. These examples showcase different funding strategies, community engagement approaches, and project scales:

  • Burlington Waterfront Park: Urban waterfront park transformation combining recreation, environmental restoration, and public access (https://enjoyburlington.com)
  • Rutland Downtown Community Park: Small-town park developed through state grants, local fundraising, and volunteer labor
  • Winooski Riverfront Trail: River access and trail development combining recreation with environmental benefits
  • Montpelier All-Abilities Playground: Inclusive playground serving diverse abilities and ages
  • Stowe Recreation Path: Multi-use trail system with 5+ miles of maintained pathways
  • Brattleboro Recreation Center & Parks: Community-driven recreation programming and facility development

Need to share your park story? Contact us to be featured!

Grant and Funding Resources

  • Vermont Recreation Grants Database: Comprehensive list of state grant programs with deadlines and application info
  • Grants.gov: Federal grant opportunities searchable by location and project type
  • City Parks Alliance Funding Hub: Searchable database of park grants nationwide
  • Foundation Center: Comprehensive database of private foundations and grants
  • Community Cats (VT): Funding resource for nonprofits in Vermont

Educational Opportunities

  • VRPA Annual Conference: Professional development for Vermont recreation professionals (typically June)
  • NRPA Webinars & Training: Online training on park planning, design, and operations
  • Regional Planning Commission Workshops: Training offered by your regional planning commission - contact your commission directly
  • UVM Extension: Community development and natural resources education programs
  • CPRP Certification: Certified Park and Recreation Professional credential

Data and Research Resources

  • Vermont Open Data Portal: Demographics and recreation participation data
  • NRPA Research: National benchmarking studies for park systems
  • ParkScore: Compare your community to other towns nationally
  • Health and Wellness Benefits Research: Studies showing health benefits of parks and outdoor recreation
  • Vermont Climate Adaptation Resources: Climate resilience and green infrastructure planning

Getting Help: Who To Contact

  • Your Regional Planning Commission: Contact by region listing with planning assistance and grant support - Find your RPC
  • Vermont Department of Forests, Parks and Recreation: State programs, technical assistance, and grant programs
  • Vermont Recreation and Parks Association (VRPA): Connections to experienced professionals and peer network
  • Vermont League of Cities and Towns: Municipal governance and development resources
  • Professional Consultants: Landscape architects, engineers, and park planners specializing in park projects
  • ParkWorks Vermont: General questions about this resource hub
