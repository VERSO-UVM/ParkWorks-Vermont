Success Stories from Vermont Towns

See how other Vermont communities have successfully created parks in their neighborhoods.

📍 Rutland

Project: Downtown Recreation Area (2022)

A small downtown park transformed community gathering with a $280,000 investment combining state grants, local fundraising, and volunteer labor. Now hosts 50+ events annually.

Key takeaway: Multi-phase funding and strong volunteer engagement enabled success.

📍 Winooski

Project: Waterfront Trail Access (2021)

Secured a $450,000 LWCF grant for riverfront park development, combining recreation with environmental restoration. Created accessible trail system for all abilities.

Key takeaway: Environmental benefits strengthen grant competitiveness.

📍 Montpelier

Project: All-Abilities Playground (2023)

Community-led effort built inclusive play space serving 2,000+ children with disabilities and their families. Raised $120,000 through local partnerships and state grants.

Key takeaway: Addressing underserved populations increases grant funding opportunities.
