Building Community Through Parks

Connect with other Vermont communities, share experiences, and learn from successful park projects across the state.

Community Engagement Strategies

  • Public Meetings: Host open forums to gather input and share project updates
  • Focus Groups: Organize targeted discussions with specific user groups (seniors, youth, families)
  • Surveys: Conduct community surveys online and in-person to assess needs and preferences
  • Design Charrettes: Interactive workshops where community members collaborate on design ideas
  • Pop-Up Events: Temporary installations or activities to test ideas and gather feedback
  • Social Media: Use Facebook, Instagram, and other platforms to engage and inform residents
  • Community Ambassadors: Recruit volunteers to spread the word and gather input

Building Partnerships

  • Schools: Partner with schools for after-school programs and field trips
  • Local Businesses: Engage businesses for sponsorships, donations, and volunteer support
  • Nonprofit Organizations: Collaborate with youth groups, service clubs, and community organizations
  • Healthcare Providers: Partner on wellness programs and healthy community initiatives
  • Conservation Groups: Work with land trusts and environmental organizations
  • Neighboring Towns: Share resources and coordinate regional recreation programs
  • State Agencies: Connect with Vermont state departments for technical assistance and funding

Volunteer Programs

  • Park Stewards: Adopt-a-park programs where individuals or groups maintain specific areas
  • Trail Maintenance: Organize volunteer trail crews for regular upkeep
  • Community Builds: Engage volunteers in construction projects like playgrounds or gardens
  • Event Volunteers: Recruit help for community events and programs
  • Youth Programs: Create youth conservation corps or service learning opportunities
  • Seasonal Cleanups: Organize spring and fall cleanup days
  • Planting Days: Host community tree and flower planting events

Programming for All Ages

  • Early Childhood: Nature play groups, parent-child programs, storytime in the park
  • Youth: Sports leagues, summer camps, outdoor adventure programs, teen activities
  • Families: Movie nights, festivals, concerts, family fun days
  • Adults: Fitness classes, walking groups, sports leagues, wellness programs
  • Seniors: Low-impact activities, social gatherings, tai chi, walking clubs
  • Special Populations: Adaptive recreation programs for people with disabilities

Creating Inclusive Communities

  • Ensure parks and programs are accessible to people of all abilities
  • Offer programs at various price points, including free options
  • Provide scholarships for families who need financial assistance
  • Welcome and celebrate cultural diversity in programming
  • Create safe, welcoming spaces for all community members
  • Engage underrepresented populations in planning and decision-making
  • Provide multilingual signage and communications where appropriate

Vermont Park and Recreation Networks

  • Vermont Recreation and Parks Association (VRPA): Statewide professional network
  • Regional Planning Commissions: Connect with other towns in your region
  • Municipal Managers: Network through Vermont League of Cities and Towns
  • Conservation Groups: Vermont Land Trust, local land trusts, and conservation commissions
  • Friends Groups: Connect with other Vermont park friends organizations

Sharing Your Success

Document and share your park projects to inspire other Vermont communities:

  • Take before and after photos of improvements
  • Track participation numbers and community engagement
  • Share lessons learned with neighboring communities
  • Present at VRPA conferences or regional meetings
  • Write articles for local newspapers and state publications
  • Post updates on social media to maintain community excitement
  • Create case studies that can help others

Events and Gatherings

  • Annual Park Celebrations: Host events to celebrate park anniversaries or grand openings
  • Seasonal Festivals: Organize spring flings, summer concerts, fall festivals, and winter carnivals
  • National Days: Participate in National Trails Day, Earth Day, or Park and Recreation Month
  • Community Picnics: Host gatherings to bring neighbors together
  • Outdoor Movies: Screen family-friendly films in the park
  • Fitness Events: Organize fun runs, bike rides, or yoga in the park

Connect With Us
