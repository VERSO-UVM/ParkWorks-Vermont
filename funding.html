Funding Your Park Project

Discover grants, fundraising strategies, and financial resources available to support Vermont park and recreation projects.

Funding Stage: Turning Plans into Reality

Securing funding is one of the most critical steps in creating a park. Fortunately, Vermont municipalities and recreation organizations have access to a variety of grants, loans, and collaborative funding opportunities. This stage will help you identify potential sources and strategies to finance your project.

1. Understand Your Funding Needs

Start by estimating the total cost of your project, including land acquisition, design, construction, and long-term maintenance. Break down costs into phases if necessary—many grants allow for incremental development.

Tip: Prepare a clear budget and timeline before applying for any funding. Most grant programs require detailed financial plans.

2. Explore Vermont-Specific Grants

Vermont offers several programs dedicated to outdoor recreation and park development:

  • Vermont Recreation Grants – Includes the Recreational Trails Program (up to $50,000, 20% match) and the Land & Water Conservation Fund (up to $1,000,000, 50% match).
  • Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF) – Supports acquisition and development of outdoor recreation facilities.
  • VOREC Community Grant Program – Funds projects that strengthen local outdoor recreation economies.
  • Vermont Recreation and Parks Association Grant List – A curated list of state and federal opportunities.

3. Look for National and Federal Programs

Beyond Vermont, there are national programs that support park and recreation projects:

  • National Recreation and Park Association (NRPA) Grants – Includes funding for youth sports, health initiatives, and community parks.
  • Outdoor Recreation Legacy Partnership (ORLP) – Competitive grants for urban and underserved communities.
  • https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/community-facilities/community-facilities-direct-loan-grant-program – Supports essential community facilities, including parks, in rural areas.
  • Rails-to-Trails Conservancy Federal Funding Guide – For trail development and active transportation projects.

4. Private Foundations and Partnerships

Private organizations often provide grants or matching funds for park projects:

  • The Great Outdoors Fund – Supports sustainable outdoor recreation through public-private partnerships.
  • City Parks Alliance Funding Hub – A resource for finding diverse funding streams for parks.
  • Trust for Public Land – Offers technical assistance and funding strategies for park creation.
  • National Recreation Foundation – Focused on youth development and outdoor access.

5. Tips for Successful Grant Applications

Most grants are competitive. Strengthen your application by:

  • Demonstrating community support through surveys and letters of endorsement.
  • Including clear project goals, timelines, and measurable outcomes.
  • Highlighting sustainability and accessibility in your design.
  • Leveraging partnerships with local nonprofits and businesses.

Consider creating a funding calendar to track deadlines and requirements for multiple grants.
