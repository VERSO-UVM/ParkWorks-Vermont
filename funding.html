Funding Your Park Project

Discover grants, fundraising strategies, and financial resources available to support Vermont park and recreation projects.

Vermont State Funding Sources

  • Vermont Recreation Facilities Grants: Annual grants from the Department of Forests, Parks and Recreation for municipal recreation facilities
  • Vermont Community Development Program (VCDP): Grants for public facilities in eligible communities
  • Municipal Planning Grants: Funding for park and recreation planning through regional planning commissions
  • Vermont Housing & Conservation Board: Grants for conservation and outdoor recreation projects
  • Ecosystem Restoration Program: Funding for projects that restore natural resources
  • Vermont Urban and Community Forestry Program: Grants for tree planting and green infrastructure

Federal Funding Programs

  • Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF): Federal funding administered by Vermont for park acquisition and development
  • Recreational Trails Program: Grants for trail development and maintenance
  • Community Development Block Grants (CDBG): Federal funding for public infrastructure in qualifying communities
  • USDA Rural Development: Grants and loans for rural community facilities
  • Transportation Alternatives Program: Funding for pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure

Private Foundation Funding

  • Vermont Community Foundation: Grants for community development projects
  • High Meadows Fund: Support for conservation and land protection
  • National Recreation and Park Association (NRPA) Grants: Various grant programs for parks nationwide
  • Local Community Foundations: Many Vermont regions have foundations supporting local projects
  • Corporate Foundations: Companies like Lowe's, Home Depot, and Target offer community grants

Community Fundraising Strategies

  • Capital Campaigns: Organized fundraising drives with specific goals and timelines
  • Friends Groups: Establish nonprofit organizations to support park development and programming
  • Naming Rights: Offer naming opportunities for benches, trees, or park features
  • Community Events: Host fundraising events like fun runs, festivals, or concerts
  • Crowdfunding: Use online platforms to reach broader donor bases
  • Business Partnerships: Partner with local businesses for sponsorships and in-kind donations
  • Memorial/Tribute Gifts: Offer opportunities for memorial donations

Municipal Funding Options

  • General Fund Appropriations: Annual budget allocations for parks and recreation
  • Bond Issues: Voter-approved bonds for major capital projects
  • Impact Fees: Developer fees dedicated to park improvements
  • Special Tax Districts: Create special service districts for park funding
  • Lease/Purchase Agreements: Financing options for equipment and facilities

Grant Writing Tips

  • Start early - many grants have annual deadlines
  • Follow application instructions precisely
  • Demonstrate community need and broad support
  • Show matching funds or in-kind contributions
  • Provide clear project budgets and timelines
  • Highlight sustainability and long-term maintenance plans
  • Include letters of support from community partners
  • Consider hiring a grant writer for large applications
